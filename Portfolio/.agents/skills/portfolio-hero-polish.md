# portfolio-hero-polish

## Purpose
Specialized polish guide for building a high-conversion, premium hero section in this portfolio.

## When to use
- Editing `src/app/page.tsx` and hero components in `src/app/components/`.
- Improving first impression and CTA clarity.
- Reworking hero copy, visuals, or card composition.

## Core rules
- Keep hero narrative clear in first viewport:
  - Identity.
  - Value proposition.
  - Primary action.
- Maintain composition balance between content and visual support.
- Keep CTA hierarchy explicit:
  - Primary CTA visually dominant.
  - Secondary CTA clearly secondary.
- Use premium depth cues carefully:
  - Layered background.
  - Controlled glow.
  - Clean glass surfaces.
- Keep hero text scannable:
  - Compact line lengths.
  - Clear heading breaks.
  - Avoid dense paragraph walls.
- Ensure hero remains strong in EN and PL.

## Checklist
- Verify hero communicates role and value in under 5 seconds.
- Verify CTA labels are specific and actionable.
- Verify hero remains readable at mobile widths.
- Verify visual side does not overpower textual content.
- Verify motion/reveal timing supports, not delays comprehension.

## Anti-patterns
- Beautiful hero with unclear value proposition.
- Overcrowded hero with too many pills/cards/labels.
- Weak CTA contrast or ambiguous button text.
- Hero visuals that look generic or stock-template.
- Mobile hero that requires excessive scrolling before CTA.

## Expected output
- Premium hero with strong first impression and clear conversion path.
- Balanced text/visual composition for both themes and locales.

## Definition of done
- Hero is immediately understandable, visually premium, and action-oriented.
- No compositional or readability regression on small screens.
